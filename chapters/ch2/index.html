Stocks & Flows: The Building Blocks

A stock is anything you can see, count, or measure at a given moment.

$$\text{Stock}(t) = \text{Stock}(t_0) + \int_{t_0}^{t} [\text{Inflow}(s) - \text{Outflow}(s)] \, ds$$

In simple terms: a stock rises when inflow exceeds outflow.

Key Insight: Stocks change slowly because flows take time.

Other Common System Traps

Fixes That Fail
A policy reduces symptoms temporarily, but has side effects.
Escalation
Two competitors each try to stay ahead, spiraling exponentially.
Drift to Low Performance
When performance drops, goals are quietly lowered to match.
Success to the Successful
Winners receive rewards that help them win even more.
