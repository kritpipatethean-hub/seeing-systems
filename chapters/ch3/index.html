The Goodwin Growth Cycle

The economy naturally oscillates.

Here's the cycle: high employment leads to higher wages.

$$\frac{d\lambda}{dt} = \lambda \left[ \frac{\kappa(1-\omega)}{v} - (\alpha + \beta) \right]$$ $$\frac{d\omega}{dt} = \omega \left[ \Phi(\lambda) - \alpha \right]$$

Minsky's Financial Instability

Think of the bathtub from Chapter 2. Debt is a stock.

Hyman Minsky identified three phases of financing.

The debt dynamics are captured in a simple stock-flow equation.

$$\frac{dD}{dt} = I - \Pi \quad \text{(Debt = Investment − Profits)}$$ $$d = \frac{D}{Y}, \quad \frac{dd}{dt} = d(r - g) + \left(\frac{I}{Y} - \frac{\Pi}{Y}\right)$$

Why SD for Economics?

Mainstream economics studies the bathtub only when the water is still.

One powerful tool is the Sectoral Balances Identity.

Another key insight from Steve Keen (2013).

$$(G - T) + (X - M) + (S - I) = 0$$
